## SECTION A ‚Äî Purpose & Philosophy

### A1. What is SSUM-Snow, in simple terms?

SSUM-Snow is a **deterministic structural trust framework** for snow forecasting.

It does not try to predict snow at all times.  
Instead, it answers a more fundamental operational question:

**‚ÄúIs it structurally safe to trust a snow forecast right now?‚Äù**

SSUM-Snow:
- does **not** force predictions
- does **not** chase magnitude
- does **not** hallucinate depth

It permits forecasts **only when structure supports trust**.

---

### A2. Why introduce structural trust in snow forecasting?

Because many real-world failures are caused by **false confidence**, not missing data.

In practice:
- snow is predicted when structure is unstable
- thresholds produce false alarms
- depth spikes appear without persistence

SSUM-Snow prevents this by **gating forecasts through structural permissibility** before any depth is considered.

---

### A3. Does SSUM-Snow replace weather models or forecasts?

No.

SSUM-Snow never replaces:
- numerical weather prediction (NWP)
- radar or ensemble systems
- classical meteorological science

It augments them by answering:

**‚ÄúShould this forecast be trusted here?‚Äù**

---

### A4. Is SSUM-Snow theoretical or philosophical?

No.

SSUM-Snow is:
- fully executable
- deterministic
- validated across multiple real stations
- traceable and auditable

Every claim is backed by **observable structural outputs**.
